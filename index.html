About Me

Born in 1992 in Gothenburg, Sweden. Growing up, computers have always been in my presence; it all started with the legendary Amiga.

The mesmerizing graphics on the Amiga got me hooked on gaming as a very young kid; a lifelong passion emerged.

This passion for games got me into programming in high school. There, I studied subjects like most high school students do. I also got the opportunity to study programming and game development subjects, which I took in a heartbeat. Here, I got introduced to both high level and low level programming languages such as Java and C++, and got to develop some games written in these languages.

After high school I ventured on to study for a Bachelor's degree in Computer Science, at Blekinge Institute of Technology in Karlskrona, Sweden. Here, I got the opportunity to learn all of the fundamentals of software development; software architecture and design, agile software development such as SCRUM, programming, testing, and so on. Towards the end I was put in a team of seven to develop a large scale game for a game project course, where I got the role as the lead graphics programmer.

When I finished my Bachelor's degree in Karlskrona, I was hungry for more knowledge; especially outside the field of game development. So I moved to Uppsala where I started my Master's degree in computer science at Uppsala University.

This opened up a whole new world for me; I discovered so many other fields within computer science, such as machine learning, artificial intelligence and concurrent- and parallel programming. Towards the end, I got the opportunity to study abroad for one semester which I did not hesitate to take. I ended up in South Korea to study Computer Science at Seoul National University--this was truly an incredible experience.

We are starting to reach an end to this story. When I came back from South Korea all I had left was to write my Master's thesis, and after some rigorous search I found an opportunity at the company King in Stockholm.

At this writing, I am currently finishing my thesis and work at King, and am then ready to venture on for the next adventure ahead!

Skyflower

Skyflower is a third person platforming game heavily inspired by 3D puzzle game cult classics such as Super Mario 64 and Banjo Kazooie.

This was part of a game development course at Blekinge Institute of Technology. I was working full-time on this in a team of seven for one semester.

The game was written completely from scratch. The engine was written in C++.

I was the lead graphics programmer for this project, and the graphics engine was written from scratch with DirectX 11.

I had the opportunity to play around with a lot of cool rendering techniques, such as skeletal animation, particle systems, depth of field, anti-aliasing , and much more!

The source code of this project is hosted on private repository on BitBucket. Get in touch with me and I will make sure you will get the complete source code if requested!

  • Date: March 2014
  • Service: Game Development

Physically-based fluid particle system using DirectCompute

This is a GPGPU project that stems from my Bachelor's thesis; research about different fluid particle system methods and evaluate them.

At the time of writing this thesis, GPGPU calculations had started to get real hot within games. What I wanted to prove with this thesis, is that water simulations on the GPGPU is feasible for real-time applications such as games.

As I saw a huge lackluster in games when it comes to water simulations, I just had to prove this!

The result the thesis was that I had found two methods: The Discrete Element Method and Smoothed Particle Hydrodynamics, with the latter resulting in a more realistic fluid simulation.

I managed to simulate up to one million particles on the GPU before falling below a framerate of 60 frames per second. I also found that the number of particles scales really well on the GPU.

The project was written from scratch in C++, particles were rendered in DirectX 11 and simulated in DirectCompute shaders.

The source code of this project can be found on my private BitBucket page.

  • Client: Blekinge Institute of Technology
  • Date: June 2014
  • Service: Bachelor's thesis

Space Adventure

Oh boy is this an old project! This is one of the first games I ever created.

The game was part of a project course I took in high school.

Space Adventure is about the adventures of Benny Barfighter; a fighter pilot with a drinking problem. You play the game by navigating in space and visit different planets. You also have to watch out for enemy ships and asteroids!

The game was written in C++ and uses the Allegro framework for basic tasks such as creating the window, processing user input, drawing the graphics, and more.
